## 13. Result-quality validation

**Status**: `decision needed` — define the v1 quality bar and the smallest test that proves it holds

The site's value depends on whether readers trust verdicts enough to argue with them. We don't currently test that property — `dr lint` checks structure, the audit trail records analyst/auditor agreement, but neither tells us whether a *published* claim is actually defensible. This item is the placeholder for that work; scope is intentionally not nailed down yet, but the v1 GA bar should at minimum specify:

**Major signals to test:**
- **Source quality and appropriateness**, not just `kind`. Today `SourceFile.kind` is one of `report | article | documentation | dataset | blog | video | index` — that's *type*, not *quality*. A press release and a peer-reviewed paper are both `article`. We have no field for primary-vs-secondary, partisan-vs-neutral, evidence-density, or "is this source appropriate to *this* claim." Either we add structured fields or we accept that quality lives only in the analyst's head.
- **Claim text fidelity to the criterion.** LLM-generated claim text is loosely controlled today: the criterion sets the topic, but the analyst may rephrase in ways that drift. Observed bad pattern: `"X is unverified"` rephrased as a verdict-word claim where the verdict then echoes the title (`unverified` → verdict `unverified`). Criterion text should constrain claim text more tightly — either templated (criterion provides the title pattern) or validated (lint rejects claim titles that lexically match the verdict word).
- **Verdict-evidence coherence.** Does the narrative actually cite the sources it claims to? Are cited quotes present in the source bodies? Does the verdict make sense given the cited stances? Today this is implicit in analyst+auditor agreement; an explicit check would catch agreed-but-wrong runs.
- **Confidence calibration.** Across a sample of published claims, does `high` confidence correlate with verdicts a reviewer would also rate `high`? `medium` and `low` similarly? Without sampling, the labels are decorative.

**Key minimums for v1 GA:**
- A documented quality bar — what makes a claim publishable beyond "lint passes and a human approved." Even if the bar is "the four signals above were spot-checked on the launch set," writing it down lets future-us tell whether we're holding the line.
- Claim text constrained by criterion (template, validator, or both). Bans the `unverified → unverified` shape.
- Some structured signal of source appropriateness — at minimum a `primary | secondary | tertiary` field, or a "claim relevance" score the analyst writes per cited source.

**Clarity for readers:**
- The bar we hold ourselves to should be stated on the site (likely in the FAQ alongside COI), so readers know what *not* having that label means about claims we declined to publish.
- The omission policy ("what's worse than publishing imperfectly?") should be operator-facing prose, not implicit in `dr lint` thresholds.

This work is not gating tag-as-1.0.0 — `1.0.0` records content/code readiness — but it is gating the "should this be visible at the public domain" decision. Plan-stub forthcoming.

**Status**: `done` (commits `0caf560`, `687ab54`, `54c09e6`, `4c15e72`, `4bb800b`, `79d69ca`, `46440c4`, `73fbbb4`, `d3c709c`, `89b6bfc`, `61470fd`, `afc86e2`, `113349c`)
