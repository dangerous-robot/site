## Energy markets

### RECs (Renewable Energy Certificates)

Tradable certificates representing the environmental attributes of one MWh of electricity generated from a renewable source. Buyers use them to claim renewable energy use on paper, even when the physical power they consume comes from the grid mix.

RECs are strongest as a credible accounting tool and a market signal. Their direct emissions impact is real but limited unless paired with additionality (the purchase causes new renewable capacity to be built that wouldn't have been otherwise) or power purchase agreements (PPAs) that fund new capacity.
