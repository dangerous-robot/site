- For context, 0.3 watt-hours is less than the amount of electricity that an LED lightbulb
  or a laptop consumes in a few minutes.
- The most important reason our estimate differs is that we use a more realistic assumption
  for the number of output tokens in typical chatbot usage.

This Gradient Updates piece revisits energy consumption estimates for ChatGPT queries using current hardware and models. The researchers used GPT-4o as reference model, accounting for mixture-of-experts architecture and H100 GPU efficiency. The analysis considers both typical short queries and longer inputs with document uploads.
