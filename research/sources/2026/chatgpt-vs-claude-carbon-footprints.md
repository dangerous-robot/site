The article presents empirical comparisons of energy efficiency between two major large language models, finding substantial per-query differences that compound at scale. It contextualizes these findings within the February 2026 OpenAI-Pentagon partnership and concurrent user migration from ChatGPT to Claude, examining how geopolitical and business developments intersect with environmental considerations. The piece also addresses broader implications of AI scaling for global electricity demand and carbon emissions trajectories through 2035.
