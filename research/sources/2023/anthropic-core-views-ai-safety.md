- AI progress may lead to transformative AI systems in the next decade, but we do
  not yet understand how to make such systems safe and aligned with human values.
- We believe that AI safety research is urgently important and should be supported
  by a wide range of public and private actors.
- So far, no one knows how to train very powerful AI systems to be robustly helpful,
  honest, and harmless.
- We believe it's hard to make rapid progress in science and engineering without close
  contact with our object of study.
- Many of our most serious safety concerns might only arise with near-human-level
  systems, and it's difficult or intractable to make progress on these problems without
  access to such AIs.

Anthropic's foundational position paper on AI safety outlines their predictions for rapid AI progress based on scaling laws, identifies technical alignment and societal disruption as key risk areas, and details their empirical research approach. The company categorizes their work into three areas: capabilities research, alignment capabilities, and alignment science, while pursuing a portfolio approach across optimistic, intermediate, and pessimistic scenarios for AI safety tractability.
