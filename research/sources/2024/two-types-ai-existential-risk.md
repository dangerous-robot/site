key_quotes:
- The accumulative hypothesis posits that AI x-risks do not exclusively materialize
  as high-magnitude events initiated by artificial general intelligence or artificial
  superintelligence.
- AI x-risks accumulate through a series of lower-severity disruptions over extensive
  duration, collectively eroding systemic resilience until some stressor event triggers
  unrecoverable collapse.

The author uses a systems analysis perspective to differentiate between the causal pathways of decisive and accumulative risks. It proposes a 'perfect storm MISTER' scenario to illustrate how social risks can compound into an existential catastrophe.
