key_quotes:
- Transparency scores ranged from 6.4% to 60.9%, with a median of 29.1%.
- Major transparency gaps included missing documentation on training data, ethical
  considerations, and limitations for deployment.
- Public documentation of authorized medical AI products in Europe lacks sufficient
  public transparency to inform about safety and risks.
- We call on lawmakers and regulators to establish legally mandated requirements for
  public and substantive transparency to fulfill the promise of trustworthy AI for
  health.

This research systematically assessed the transparency of commercially available medical AI products in Europe using a 55-question survey framework. The study found significant gaps in public documentation regarding algorithmic development, ethical considerations, and deployment limitations across CE-certified AI tools for radiology.
