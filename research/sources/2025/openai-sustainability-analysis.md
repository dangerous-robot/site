summary: Comprehensive analysis of OpenAI's environmental practices, finding limited

key_quotes:
- OpenAI is the most prominent AI research company in the world, yet it remains among
  the least transparent in its environmental reporting.
- Third-party analysis by DitchCarbon gave OpenAI, Inc. a climate score of just 23
  out of 100, citing the absence of specific emissions figures and no formally documented
  reduction targets.
- Training GPT-3 produced approximately 502 metric tons of CO2 equivalent, while training
  GPT-4 is estimated to have generated between 12,000 and 15,000 metric tons of CO2
  equivalent.
- OpenAI has published zero verified Scope 1, 2, or 3 figures as of March 2026
- The single largest challenge facing OpenAI on sustainability is the complete absence
  of a formal emissions reporting framework.

This detailed sustainability assessment examines OpenAI's environmental practices across 18 categories including carbon emissions, water usage, and energy consumption. The analysis reveals significant gaps in environmental transparency and accountability compared to industry peers like Microsoft, Google, and Meta.
