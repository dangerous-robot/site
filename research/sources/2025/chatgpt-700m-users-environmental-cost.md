summary: Examines ChatGPT's growth to 700 million weekly users and analyzes environmental

key_quotes:
- Each time a user enters a prompt into ChatGPT, servers housed in large data centers
  activate to generate a response.
- Recent research shows that each ChatGPT query consumes about 0.3 to 0.4 watt-hours
  of electricity.
- Estimates show that daily inference needs more than 340 megawatt-hours (MWh) of
  electricity. That's about the same as what 30,000 U.S. homes use in a day.
- A study estimates that every 20 to 50 queries to ChatGPT uses about half a liter
  of water.
- Globally, the AI industry is expected to draw 4.2 to 6.6 billion cubic meters of
  water per year by 2027 if growth continues at the current pace.

This article analyzes the environmental footprint of ChatGPT as it reaches 700 million weekly active users, representing a four-fold increase from the previous year. The piece examines carbon emissions, energy consumption, and water usage associated with AI inference and model training.
