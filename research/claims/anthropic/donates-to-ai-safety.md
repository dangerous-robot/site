Multiple independent sources show that Anthropic provides money to organizations and programs that focus on AI safety. In 2023 the company contributed **$20 million** to Public First Action, a 501(c)(4) whose stated purpose is public education on AI policy and the promotion of safe and responsible AI; the donation was expressly restricted so it could not be used for election influence (Transformer, *Anthropic’s donations can’t be used to influence elections — despite what everyone thought*). Anthropic also runs the **Anthropic Fellows Program**, a pilot that offers funding and mentorship to a small cohort of researchers to work full‑time on AI‑safety research topics such as scalable oversight and adversarial robustness (Anthropic, *Introducing the Anthropic Fellows Program*; Anthropic, *Anthropic Fellows Program for AI safety research: applications open for May & July 2026*). These fellowship grants constitute direct financial support for AI‑safety research. Together, the corporate charitable contribution and the fellowship funding demonstrate that Anthropic does indeed donate to AI‑safety causes.
