Microsoft’s governance materials repeatedly reference its shareholders and the need to protect their long‑term interests (Corporate Governance Policies and Guidelines; Corporate Governance Framework). The company’s ownership charts are compiled from filings such as the annual Form 10‑K and the proxy statement (Form 14A) – documents that are required only of companies whose shares are listed on a public exchange. These disclosures confirm that Microsoft is a publicly‑traded corporation (Microsoft Organization Charts – Ownership Structure and Governance). Therefore, among the listed options (publicly‑traded, privately‑held, non‑profit, B‑corp, employee‑owned, steward‑ownership, cooperative), the applicable classification for Microsoft is “publicly‑traded.”
