The claim that the majority of Microsoft's renewable energy and carbon‑neutral pledges rely on carbon credits or other financial contributions is not substantiated by the available sources. While Microsoft does invest in carbon‑removal projects and has purchased removal credits (as noted in its Carbon Removal Program and the recent pause on carbon‑removal purchases), it also engages in large‑scale direct renewable procurement. The Historic Renewable Energy Power Purchase Agreement with Brookfield outlines a commitment to deliver over 10.5 GW of renewable power capacity, representing one of the largest corporate PPAs ever signed. These sources illustrate a mixed approach of direct renewable sourcing and financial mechanisms, but none provide quantitative evidence that *most* of Microsoft's pledges are fulfilled through credits. Consequently, the assertion that the majority of its pledges involve carbon credits cannot be verified from the provided material.
