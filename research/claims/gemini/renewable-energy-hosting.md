Google states that its data centers have maintained a 100 % renewable‑energy match on a global basis every year since 2017, and it reports very low per‑prompt emissions for Gemini (0.03 g CO₂e per median text prompt) using a market‑based accounting method that reflects those renewable purchases (Source 2; Source 1). However, an independent analysis by Greenly notes that the market‑based approach may understate actual emissions, estimating that a Gemini 2.5 Pro prompt could emit about 7.4 g CO₂e, suggesting that the model’s hosting may not be fully powered by renewable sources in practice (Source 3). Consequently, while Google’s own reporting supports the claim that Gemini is hosted on renewable‑matched energy, external critique indicates the evidence is not conclusive, leading to a mixed assessment.
