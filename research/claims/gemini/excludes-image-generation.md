The claim that Gemini excludes image generation is contradicted by multiple official Google sources. The Gemini API documentation explicitly states that "Gemini can generate and process images conversationally with text, images, or a combination of both" and describes native image-generation models such as Nano Banana and Gemini 3 Pro Image (Gemini API). Google Cloud’s Gemini image-generation limits page details the supported languages, input constraints, and safety rules for the image-generation models, confirming that the feature exists and is subject to specific limitations (Gemini image generation limitations). Additionally, the Nano Banana 2 product page promotes the "Gemini AI image generator & photo editor" and notes that AI image generation is available wherever the Gemini app is offered (Nano Banana 2 - Gemini AI image generator & photo editor). An external overview of Gemini 3 Pro Image further describes it as Google’s flagship AI image model, highlighting its ability to create images with text rendering and synthetic watermarks (What Is Gemini 3 Pro Image? Google's Flagship AI Image Model). Collectively, these sources demonstrate that Gemini does provide image-generation functionality, making the claim false.
