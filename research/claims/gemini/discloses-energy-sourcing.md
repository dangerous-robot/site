Google has released environmental impact data showing that the median Gemini AI text prompt consumes 0.24 Wh of energy and uses 0.03 gCO2e. However, while Google reports that these metrics have improved over time, reports also note an increase in overall greenhouse gas emissions (48% since 2019), indicating that despite efficiency gains, renewable energy does not account for 100% of Gemini’s infrastructure and operations. Primary source: Google’s acknowledged 2024 environmental report and independent analyses (Sources 3, 4, and 8), which highlight the efficiency of individual prompts but acknowledge broader emissions growth. The key information supporting this is that the total energy usage from data centers and AI systems has increased, countering the claim of total reliance on renewable sources. Source 8 directly outlines the 48% growth in emissions from 2019, with AI mentioned as a key driver. Further, while Google’s blog posts (Source 4 and 6) trumpet efficiency achievements, they do not explicitly claim 100% renewable energy for Gemini’s total footprint. However, it's worth noting that Google does have initiatives to move towards 100% renewable data centers by 2030 and has made progress in reducing the carbon intensity of its operations over time, though this title oversimplifies the current status.
