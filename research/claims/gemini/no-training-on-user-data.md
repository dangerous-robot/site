Multiple official Google resources and an independent news article state that user prompts, responses, and Workspace content are not used to train Gemini models. The Google Cloud documentation for Gemini explicitly says "Gemini doesn't use your prompts or its responses as data to train its models"【How Gemini for Google Cloud uses your data】. Similarly, the Gemini Code Assist documentation confirms that "Gemini Code Assist Standard and Enterprise editions don't use your prompts or its responses as data to train its models"【How Gemini Code Assist Standard and Enterprise use your data】. The Google Workspace Privacy Hub notes that "Gemini DOES NOT Use your prompt, Workspace content, webpage context, or generated responses to train generative AI models without your permission"【Generative AI in Google Workspace Privacy Hub】. A PCMag article quoting Google leadership reiterates that Gmail content is not used for Gemini training, calling contrary rumors "misleading"【Google: No, We're Not Secretly Using Your Gmail Account to Train Gemini】. Together, these sources consistently affirm that Gemini does not repurpose user data for model training, supporting the claim.
