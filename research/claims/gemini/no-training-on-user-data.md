Multiple Google-published sources state that Gemini does not use prompts, responses, or Workspace content to train its underlying models without explicit permission. The Google Cloud data-governance page explicitly says "Gemini doesn't use your prompts or its responses as data to train its models" (Source 1). The Google Workspace privacy page adds that "We do not use your Workspace data to train or improve the underlying generative AI… without permission" and "Gemini … doesn't store your prompt or the generated output without your permission" (Source 2). The Workspace Help article reiterates that Gemini "does NOT use your data for training generative AI models outside your domain without permission" (Source 3). A PCMag article quoting Google's response to rumors confirms that Gmail content is not used to train Gemini (Source 4). Across these independent statements, there is consistent assurance that user data is not used for model training unless the user consents, supporting the claim.
