According to Google’s own reporting (Sources 2, 3, and 7), Gemini does provide metrics on average energy and water usage per query, but these are averaged, not real-time values. These metrics are calculated over thousands of queries, not based on actual moment-to-moment measurement of individual requests. There is no evidence the system publicly displays individual user queries' energy consumption in real-time. The released energy data for Gemini is a median per prompt, not a live reading.

Sources 1 and 4, from outside Google, report that results are widely shared but still aggregated. Source 7’s comprehensive methodology shows the company assesses environmental impact over batches and models, not per use case. Source 6 (Community forum) and banners in Source 5 ⸻ a newsletter about multiple topics ⸻ do not directly address real-time energy insight.

If "real-time energy usage" in this claim implies the Google user is given a live energy metric, **this is not supported by evidence**. However, the claim may have conflated access to aggregate or average data with real-time metrics, which are distinct. Confidence in this verdict is capped at medium: while well-covered, all sources remain self-reported (Google-owned, affiliated, or restated from Google sources). Some reports note significant efficiency gains and environmental impact but do not guarantee individualized, instant feedback to users.
