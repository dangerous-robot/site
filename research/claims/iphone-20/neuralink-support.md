Apple has added brain-computer interface support to iOS through its Switch Control accessibility framework, but specifically through a partnership with Synchron, not Neuralink. According to "You Can Now Control Apple Devices With a Brain Implant," Apple's BCI integration uses Synchron's Stentrode implant, which is inserted through blood vessels rather than requiring direct brain surgery. "Apple Preparing to Launch Mind-Control Support for iPhones" confirms Apple is pursuing BCI technology through Synchron's less invasive approach, contrasting with Neuralink's more invasive surgical implantation method. While Apple has demonstrated commitment to BCI technology, there is no evidence in the sources of any partnership or support specifically for Neuralink devices.
