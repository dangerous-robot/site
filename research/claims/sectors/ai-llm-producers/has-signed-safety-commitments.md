In March 2023, the Future of Life Institute (FLI) published an open letter calling for a six-month pause on training AI systems more powerful than GPT-4. The letter gathered over 30,000 signatures, including executives and researchers from Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and other major AI/LLM producers. In November 2023, at the UK AI Safety Summit held at Bletchley Park, 28 nations and the leading frontier AI companies -- including Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and OpenAI -- signed the Frontier AI Safety Commitments. Those commitments require signatories to publish safety frameworks, conduct pre-deployment risk assessments, and share safety information with governments and each other under defined circumstances.

Both the FLI letter and the Bletchley commitments are verified as real instruments with named signatories from the major AI/LLM producers. The signing itself is documented. Confidence is medium rather than high for two reasons: the commitments are voluntary with no enforcement mechanism, and compliance is self-assessed. The FLI letter committed to nothing binding; "signing" it was a public statement, not a contractual obligation. The Bletchley commitments carry more structure -- they were coordinated by the UK government and require published safety frameworks -- but there is no independent auditor and no penalty for non-compliance.

As of the as-of date, the major AI/LLM producers (Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Microsoft, OpenAI) have each published materials described as safety or preparedness frameworks, partially satisfying the Bletchley commitment. Whether those frameworks meet the spirit of the commitment is contested. The verdict is true in the narrow sense that leading producers have signed these instruments; the wider question of whether the commitments are being meaningfully honored is not addressed here.
