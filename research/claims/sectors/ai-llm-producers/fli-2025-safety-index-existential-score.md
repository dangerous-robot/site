Multiple independent reports from the Future of Life Institute (FLI) confirm that none of the seven leading AI/LLM producers assessed—Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, xAI, Meta, Zhipu AI, and DeepSeek—received a grade above D in the ‘Existential Safety’ domain of the 2025 AI Safety Index. The FLI summary explicitly states that “none of the companies scrutinised had anything like a coherent, actionable plan” and that “no company achieved a score above a ‘D’ in this domain for the second consecutive edition.” These findings are echoed in secondary coverage: The Guardian reports that “none of the companies … scored above a D in Existential Safety planning,” and Quantum Zeitgeist notes that “no company achieved a score above a ‘D’ in this domain.” Because the claim that AI/LLM producers score D or worse on the index’s existential‑threat metric is directly supported by these sources, the claim is true with high confidence.
