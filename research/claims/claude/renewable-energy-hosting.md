There is no direct evidence in first-party, independent, or third-party sources indicating that Claude AI is hosted using renewable energy. Environmental benchmarks from Source 2 (Devera, 2025) and Source 1 (CarbonCredits 2026) indicate that Claude 3.7 has a significantly higher energy consumption per request (4.05 Wh with ~1.80 g CO₂ per query) compared to more energy-efficient models like GPT-4 (0.30 Wh and 0.13 g CO₂ per query). Only Source 3 – a first-party Anthropic blog (2026/aes)—mentions Claude as used by AES on Google Cloud's Vertex AI, but does not specify energy sources. GreenPT (Source 6, 2026) highlights its own use of 100% renewable energy while comparing itself to Claude, implying that Claude does not use renewable energy based on the comparison. However, third-party sources (as in Sources 1 and 2) and third-party ESG databases (the absence of which is noted in Sources 2 and 3) do not address Claude's hosting energy specifically. While there is no direct contradiction to the claim, the holistic evidence does not support the notion of renewable energy hosting. The data gaps and comparison with less sustainable models lead to a 'false' verdict, balanced by 'medium' confidence due to the lack of direct contrary evidence but the presence of indirect indicators suggesting higher energy usage.
