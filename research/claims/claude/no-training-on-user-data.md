Anthropic's official documentation confirms that Claude does use user data for model training, but only with explicit user consent. According to 'Is my data used for model training?', Anthropic will use chats and coding sessions to improve Claude if users choose to allow it. The 'Updates to Consumer Terms and Privacy Policy' and 'Updates to Our Privacy Policy' documents specify that this is an opt-in system where users maintain control through Privacy Settings. However, the WIRED article 'Anthropic Will Use Claude Chats for Training Data' notes that users who do not opt out will have their conversations used for training, indicating a default-to-inclusion model for consumer users as of October 2025. Incognito chats are explicitly excluded from training regardless of settings. The claim's phrasing as a negation is contradicted by the evidence showing Claude does use user data for training under the described conditions.
