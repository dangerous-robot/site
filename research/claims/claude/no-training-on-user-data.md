Anthropic’s public policy states that for its commercial products (e.g., Claude for Work, API, Claude Gov) user inputs and outputs are not used for model training unless the user explicitly opts in via feedback mechanisms (Source 1). However, for consumer‑facing Claude products (Free, Pro, Max) Anthropic introduced a policy change that defaults to using chat logs for training unless users opt out, with data retained for up to five years (Source 2, Source 3, Source 4). Users can disable this data use through privacy settings, but the default setting is opt‑in, and the policy applies to most consumer users. Therefore, the blanket claim that "Claude does not use user data for training" is inaccurate for the primary consumer offerings, making the claim false overall.
