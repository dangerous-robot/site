The claim that Claude excludes frontier-scale models is contradicted by multiple authoritative sources. According to 'Introducing Claude Opus 4.6,' Claude Opus 4.6 'outperforms the industry's next-best model (OpenAI's GPT-5.2) by around 144 Elo points on GDPval-AA,' positioning it as a frontier model by performance standards. The 'Models overview' from Anthropic describes Claude Opus 4.7 as 'our most capable generally available model for complex reasoning and agentic coding.' Microsoft's 'Introducing Anthropic's Claude models in Microsoft Foundry' explicitly classifies Claude models as 'frontier models' available alongside GPT frontier models. The sources consistently present Claude's Opus variants (4.7, 4.6) as state-of-the-art, frontier-tier language models with industry-leading performance across multiple benchmarks.
