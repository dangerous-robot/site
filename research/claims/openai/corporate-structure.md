All of the sources describe OpenAI’s current legal form as a **Public Benefit Corporation (PBC)** that is not listed on any stock exchange.  The company’s for‑profit arm, OpenAI Group, is a Delaware‑registered PBC that raised billions of dollars from private investors such as Microsoft, but there is no indication that its shares are publicly traded.  Because it is not listed on a public market, OpenAI is correctly classified as a **privately‑held** company.  

- NBC News notes that the new structure “allows investors … to more easily reap returns… and paves the way to an envisioned public offering,” implying the company is **currently private** and not yet publicly listed【Source 1】.- PYMNTS describes the transition to a “public benefit corporation” and discusses legal tensions, without mentioning any public market listing【Source 2】.- Revenue Memo explicitly states that OpenAI “completed its conversion … to a full for‑profit Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) in mid‑2025” and details private equity stakes held by Microsoft and employees【Source 3】.- AI Funding Tracker lists ownership percentages (Microsoft 27 %, OpenAI Foundation 26 %, employees 25 %) and again calls the entity a “Public Benefit Corporation (PBC)”, confirming it is a **private** corporate vehicle【Source 5】.

No source reports that OpenAI is publicly‑traded, a B‑Corp, employee‑owned, steward‑owned, or a cooperative.  Therefore the only accurate option among the list is **privately‑held**.
