According to "Profits and nonprofits: the odd evolution of OpenAI," OpenAI has undergone multiple corporate restructuring efforts since its 2015 founding as a tax-exempt nonprofit charity. The company has adopted a public benefit corporation (PBC) model, which differs from traditional corporations by allowing the balancing of shareholder profits with broader stakeholder interests. While OpenAI fits the "publicly-traded, privately-held, non-profit, B-corp, employee-owned, steward-ownership, cooperative" framework most closely as a hybrid structure combining nonprofit and benefit corporation elements, the exact current legal status requires clarification between PBC and B-corp designations.
