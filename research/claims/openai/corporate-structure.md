OpenAI is not listed on any stock exchange and has not completed an initial public offering. Its current legal form is a **public benefit corporation (PBC)** that is wholly owned by its non‑profit parent, the OpenAI nonprofit foundation. Sources describe the organization as a "capped, for‑profit" entity that operates under a non‑profit‑controlled LLC, and note that the company is considering an IPO in the future but remains privately held today. This matches the definition of a privately‑held company and rules out the other listed structures (publicly‑traded, B‑corp, employee‑owned, steward‑ownership, cooperative, or a pure non‑profit). 

- The Midas Project & Tech Oversight Project analysis notes the shift to a for‑profit public benefit corporation and the removal of nonprofit oversight, confirming the for‑profit, privately‑held nature of the entity【Restructuring Concerns】.
- FourWeekMBA explains that OpenAI’s structure consists of a single‑member Delaware LLC (OpenAI, Inc.) controlled by the non‑profit foundation and a capped‑profit LP, both of which are not publicly traded【OpenAI's Complex Structure: Foundation vs LLC Explained】.
- PYMNTS reports that OpenAI’s conversion to a public benefit corporation “could make it easier to raise funds” but does not indicate any public listing, highlighting the company’s continued private status【OpenAI’s New Corporate Structure Sets Up a Tangled Legal Future】.

Thus, the claim that OpenAI’s corporate structure is privately‑held is well supported by multiple independent sources.
