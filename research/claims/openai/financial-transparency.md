OpenAI's financial transparency has evolved significantly over time. The company filed public Form 990 tax documents during its nonprofit phase, as shown in ProPublica's database of OpenAI's 2017 filing. However, according to WIRED's reporting, OpenAI 'quietly scrapped a promise to disclose key documents to the public' and reversed its 7-year policy of making governing documents publicly available. The company now states it 'provides financial statements when requested' and aligns with industry standards, but no longer publicly distributes internal documents as of 2022. This represents a shift from the company's founding commitment to transparency to more typical corporate practices.
