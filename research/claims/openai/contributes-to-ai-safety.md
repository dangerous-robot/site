OpenAI publicly runs a suite of AI‑safety initiatives, including its Safety & Responsibility hub, the “How we think about safety and alignment” page, and detailed safety‑protocol documentation that describe RLHF fine‑tuning, adversarial testing, rate limits, content filters, and child‑safety measures (sources 4, 5, 6, 3). It also launched an external AI‑safety research fellowship that provides compute, a stipend, and a workspace for scholars to work on safety‑related topics (source 1). Recent product updates such as parental controls and distress‑notification features for ChatGPT further illustrate its commitment to safe deployments (source 8). However, none of the reviewed sources report direct financial donations or grants to external AI‑safety NGOs or labs; the only source that mentions OpenAI‑related funding (source 2) describes a policy coalition rather than a grant. Consequently, while OpenAI clearly contributes through programs, partnerships, and safety‑focused product features, evidence of monetary contributions to external safety organisations is lacking.
