OpenAI publicly discusses a range of environmental initiatives, including making its models more energy‑efficient (e.g., quantization and pruning that cut query power use to ~0.3 Wh) and running inference on Microsoft Azure’s renewable‑energy‑sourced cloud infrastructure; these efforts are described in its own “Environmental Impact of AI” article and highlighted in an independent Medium piece on AI carbon‑footprint reductions. OpenAI also reports on its carbon emissions and energy consumption through its Trust Portal and various sustainability‑related disclosures, though a Guardian report notes that future models such as GPT‑5 may have substantially higher energy demands. Independent ESG and sustainability ratings (illuminem’s OpenAI Sustainability Performance and BrandImpact’s OpenAI Brand Sustainability Scoring) list OpenAI’s environmental metrics and give it a low industry rank, suggesting modest overall performance. No source was found that documents direct financial donations or grants to environmental NGOs, so that aspect of OpenAI’s contribution remains unverified.
