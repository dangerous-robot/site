GreenPT's official documentation (Sources 1–5) explicitly states it uses renewable energy, local, and self-hosted infrastructure while presenting smaller and highly efficient models. The platform emphasizes sustainability and claims to avoid over-reliance on large-scale computing power (Source 4: quantised AI models), in line with the GreenPT design (Source 2: renewable energy, low PUE/WUE). The proposed mWh per 100 tokens metric (Source 3: new AI metric) indicates a focus on reducing energy overhead and aligns with `smaller and no frontier models`. Source 6's API docs outline models like GreenL and GreenR (no mention of 'frontier'), and Source 7's pricing does not rely on compute using large-scale infrastructure. These sources collectively confirm no evidence of using high-capacity models; economic tiers (Source 7) also do not indicate associated with costly high-compute setups. The direct exclusion is not in the form of language defining 'frontier-scale', but multiple cases are self-evidently incompatible with such models. Additionally, European AI regulation and ethical compliance often implicitly apply, further supporting this conclusion.
