GreenPT’s own website and documentation describe a platform that is hosted on European data centres powered by verified renewable energy, that measures real‑time CO₂ and energy usage, and that avoids external API calls to minimise its footprint【2025/greenpt】. The company’s technical pages list concrete sustainability metrics such as low Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) and Water Usage Effectiveness (WUE) values【2025/greenpt-vs-chatgpt】 and outline a metric of milliwatt‑hours per 100 tokens in a slide‑deck report【2025/08keusgreenptpdf】. An independent AI‑focused news article confirms these practices, noting that GreenPT’s infrastructure relies on self‑hosted open‑source models, renewable‑energy‑sourced servers, and even attempts to reuse server heat to reduce environmental impact【2025/greenpt-builds-ai-chat-platform-on-european-renewable-infrastructure】. While GreenPT’s materials position the service as a contributor to sustainability and no source refutes these claims, third‑party assessments do not provide quantified impact data or external ESG ratings. Consequently, the claim that GreenPT contributes to environmental causes is supported by both the company’s statements and an independent description of its sustainable architecture, but the evidence lacks independent measurement of actual environmental outcomes.
