OpenAI provides public documentation that lists the models available to power ChatGPT and indicates which models correspond to each subscription tier. The OpenAI‑hosted "All models" page enumerates every model on the platform, including the newer GPT‑5 series and the open‑weight variants (Source 1). In addition, third‑party guides such as DataNorth’s "ChatGPT models: What are the models and when to use them?" explain that Free, Plus, Pro, and Business plans each map to specific model families (e.g., the reasoning‑oriented o‑series or the multimodal GPT‑5 series) (Source 2). BleepingComputer also references an internal OpenAI document titled “ChatGPT Enterprise – Models & Limits” that outlines which model (e.g., GPT‑4o, GPT‑4.5, o3) is employed for particular tasks (Source 3). Together, these sources demonstrate that OpenAI openly communicates the underlying model options and their allocation across plans, satisfying the claim that ChatGPT’s ecosystem discloses which models it uses. The claim is not fully verified at the level of a real‑time UI indicator for each individual chat, but the public documentation of model assignments is clear, making the overall statement mostly true.
