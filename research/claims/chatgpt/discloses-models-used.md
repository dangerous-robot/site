OpenAI publicly lists the models that power its ChatGPT service. The OpenAI documentation pages enumerate the current model families—such as the GPT‑5.5, GPT‑5.4, and the GPT‑5.2 series—and describe their capabilities, pricing, and intended use cases (Source 1, Source 2). A dedicated overview of ChatGPT’s available models specifies that the “current ChatGPT lineup centers on the GPT‑5.2 family” and explains selectable modes that let users choose between speed, depth, or research‑grade performance (Source 4). These sources show that OpenAI explicitly tells users which model families are behind ChatGPT, thereby meeting the claim that ChatGPT discloses which models it uses.
