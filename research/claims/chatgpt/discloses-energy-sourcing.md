OpenAI, the parent company of ChatGPT, has not published explicit details about its energy sourcing for ChatGPT in its primary documentation and transparency reports. However, third-party analyses estimate that ChatGPT's inference likely requires significant resources, with some sources pointing to the need for data center infrastructure that may not be certified as using renewable energy at all times. For instance, OpenAI has announced data center projects that are expected to be operational by 2025, but these plans do not specify primary energy sourcing. Claims from third-party analyses [Source 2, Source 7] estimate high energy usage per prompt but do not confirm renewable energy sources. Recent articles [Source 3, Source 6] suggest that GPT-5 models are likely not running on 100% renewable energy and energy needs have significantly increased with model size. Source 4 reveals that most LLM compute is data center-based and likely powered primarily by grid energy, which is not exclusively renewable.

**Note**: The company's disclosure policy limits transparency on specific energy sources for ChatGPT's infrastructure, leaving independent analyses to infer based on industry benchmarks. OpenAI has not publicly confirmed if ChatGPT runs on renewable energy, leading to the 'false' verdict.  However, due to the lack of direct, publicly available primary information and only partially complementary analyses from authorities like MIT [Source 4], the confidence is capped at 'medium' due to reliance on indirect estimates.
