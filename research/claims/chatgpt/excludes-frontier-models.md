Sources directly contradict the claim that ChatGPT excludes frontier-scale models. ChatGPT's current offerings, particularly GPT-4 and its successive versions, are representative of frontier-sized AI models, with parameter counts comparable to other large models like those from Hugging Face's Hugging Face Educational and Anthropic's model lineups. Key sources (e.g., 2025/250218505, 2025/gpt-45-not-a-frontier-model) specifically mention that GPT-4.5, the latest release from OpenAI, was explicitly labeled as a large-scale model by the company, contradicting the claim that it is excluded from being termed 'frontier-scale'. Furthermore, the notion of 'frontier' generally refers to models that are pushing the limits of current scaling techniques and capabilities—not models that are out of development but rather current or imminent in production. ChatGPT's scale and computational demands align with this definition. Open-source comparisons like the 2025/analysis-llm-transparency-accessibility survey do not exclude ChatGPT from the frontier category; rather, they discuss the broader lack of true open-source transparency in all such models. Industry and community discussions (e.g., from r/ChatGPTPro and Hacker News) note that while GPT-4.5 may not be revolutionary, it remains a large and scaled model. Multiple sources also clarify that the term 'frontier' refers to the evolving edge in AI capabilities rather than a size threshold that ChatGPT would be excluded from.
