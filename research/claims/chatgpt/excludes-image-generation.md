ChatGPT's official documentation and technical specifications, confirmed by external reviews (e.g., `The Top 10 Limitations of ChatGPT` and `How to Navigate the Limitations of ChatGPT Effectively`), do not describe image generation as a built-in feature. OpenAI's CEO, Sam Altman, has also publicly acknowledged technical and policy limitations, even during temporary overuse turmoil in early 2025. Various third-party sources, including a 2024 blog on multimodal AI (`Single Grain`) and technical analysis like `How to use the new image generation AI via API?` on Reddit, clarify that while some experimental or external integrations (**not directly part of ChatGPT core capabilities**) may offer basic image generation, the product itself—by design—does not prioritize this function. The core language model's intended use (text-based dynamic query and response) and divergent technical architecture point to a deliberate exclusion of advanced imagery generation. Most evidence confirms this intended design feature gap.

However, key takeaway: conversational limitations, including lack of image generation, are mentioned as trade-offs in model design.
