Google’s own publications describe a range of AI safety initiatives. The 2023 AI Principles Progress Update outlines governance frameworks and safety measures (AI Principles 2023 Progress Update). The Google AI safety page lists the SAIF framework, red‑team testing, watermarking tools such as SynthID, and notes that in 2023 the company awarded $10 million to more than 600 researchers (AI Safety). The AI for Privacy, Safety, and Security research award program funds university projects to improve digital safety and adversarial‑AI mitigation (AI for Privacy, Safety, and Security). The 2023 Ads Safety Report details AI‑driven enforcement that blocked 5.5 billion harmful ads, showing operational use of AI for safety (Ads Safety Report 2023). Additional reports on responsible AI for child sexual abuse prevention describe detection tools, partnerships, and policy actions (Responsible AI and Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation Online). Together, these first‑party sources indicate that Google actively contributes to AI safety research, tooling, and policy. However, no independent ESG or responsible‑AI assessments were found that evaluate Google’s contributions, leaving an external validation gap (sq4).
