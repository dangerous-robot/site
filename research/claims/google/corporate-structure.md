According to the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) filings indexed by AlphaResearch, Google operates as a publicly-traded company under its parent Alphabet Inc., traded on NASDAQ under the symbol GOOGL (source: 2026/all-filings / 2026/goog). This is further corroborated by Lexchart's corporate structure charts (2026/google) based on 10-K filings, which detail Alphabet Inc. incorporated primarily as a Delaware corporation with a dual-class voting structure (source: 2026/google). Additionally, the article 'Who Really Owns Google (Alphabet) and Who Controls It' (source 2023/who-owns-google-alphabet) clarifies that despite holding less than 50% of the total shares, founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin control 51.2% of voting power through super-voting shares (Class C stock, non-publicly tradeable). This structure is typical of publicly traded companies, as classified by the Securities and Exchange Commission. However, while there is no evidence that Google or Alphabet qualifies as a B-Corp, employee-owned, or cooperative (as indicated by absence of relevant documentation or statements), its publicly-traded status is unequivocal. Regulatory filings (e.g., 2026/goog) and corporate documents (2026/google) provide consistent evidence for this conclusion.
