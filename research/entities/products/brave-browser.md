Brave Browser emerged in 2016 as an alternative to mainstream browsers, founded on a commitment to user privacy and data security. Conceived by Brendan Eich, the creator of JavaScript and co-founder of Mozilla Firefox, Brave differentiated itself by built-in ad and tracker blocking, aiming to reduce unwanted surveillance and Privacy Enhanced Technologies (PETs). The browser's launch included core features like the ad-blocker Shield and integration with the Basic Attention Token (BAT), a cryptocurrency designed to compensate content creators while circumventing traditional ad-driven revenue models.

From its early versions, Brave evolved to incorporate additional privacy tools, such as fingerprinting resistance and secure DNS. Its AI integration was introduced later as part of ongoing efforts to enhance user control over digital experiences. By deprecating support for Flash Player and Chrome extensions in favor of its own privacy-safe alternatives, Brave began consolidating its position as a standalone solution. The browser expanded cross-platform availability, initially targeting desktop environments before later adapting to mobile, which further cemented its appeal among privacy advocates and tech-savvy users seeking greater autonomy over their data.
